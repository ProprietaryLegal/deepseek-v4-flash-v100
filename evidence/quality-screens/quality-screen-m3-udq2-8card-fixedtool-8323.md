## hearsay [OK] (37.5s)
**Prompt:** Explain in two sentences why a text message offered to prove the truth of its contents is hearsay, and name one exception that might admit it.

```
A text message offered to prove the truth of what it asserts is hearsay because it is an out-of-court written statement, and the party offering it is using the words themselves as proof of the matter declared—precisely the situation the hearsay rule targets. The message lacks the safeguards of in-court testimony: the declarant is not under oath, cannot be cross-examined, and the fact-finder cannot observe the declarant's demeanor while making the statement.

One exception that might admit it is the **present sense impression** exception (Federal Rule of Evidence 803(1)), which covers a statement describing or explaining an event made while or immediately after the declarant perceived it—so a text sent contemporaneously with what it describes (e.g., "There's a car crash at 5th and Main right now") could come in for its truth.
```
